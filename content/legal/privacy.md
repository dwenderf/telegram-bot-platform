# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 21 July 2026**

Leguan ("**Leguan**", "**we**", "**us**", or "**our**") is a service that adds an AI assistant to Telegram group chats. Leguan is operated by [KENNTNIS LEGAL ENTITY NAME], [registered address], [jurisdiction of incorporation].

This policy explains what personal data Leguan processes, why, on what basis, who it is shared with, how long it is kept, and the choices and rights you have. It applies to the Leguan bot in Telegram groups and to the Leguan management dashboard.

If you have questions about this policy or want to exercise your rights, contact us at **[privacy@leguan.ai]**.

---

## 1. Who is responsible for your data (controllers and processors)

Leguan is a multi-tenant service. Each team, organization, or individual that adds the Leguan bot to a Telegram group and configures it (the "**Workspace Administrator**") decides why and how the bot is used in that group. For the messages and content in that group, the **Workspace Administrator is the data controller**, and **Leguan acts as a data processor** on their behalf — we process that content according to their instructions and to provide the service.

For our own operational data — the accounts of dashboard users, service logs, and information needed to run and secure the platform — **Leguan is the data controller**.

If the bot is active in a group you belong to, and you want to know who administers it or wish to raise a request with them, you can ask the group's administrators, or contact us and we will help route your request.

---

## 2. What data we process

Depending on how you interact with Leguan, we process:

- **Group messages and content.** When the bot is present in a group (with Telegram "privacy mode" off, which the service requires to function), it receives and stores the text of messages sent in that group so it can provide features such as question-answering, recaps, and saved context. This may include any personal data that group members choose to include in their messages.
- **Telegram identifiers and metadata.** Associated with messages, we process identifiers such as your Telegram user ID, username or display name, the group/chat ID, and the topic/thread and timestamp of a message.
- **Documents you share with the bot.** If a group member sends or references a document for the bot to read, its contents are processed to generate a response.
- **Dashboard account data.** If you use the management dashboard, we process your email address and authentication data, and records of the workspaces and groups you manage.
- **Technical and security data.** Standard operational logs (for example, event and error logs) used to run, debug, and secure the service.

We do **not** ask for, and ask that you do not send, sensitive personal data (such as health, biometric, or government-identifier information) through the bot. Because group chat is open-ended, we cannot prevent it being typed; where it appears, our lawful basis and data-minimization practices (below) apply, and you may request its removal.

---

## 3. Why we process it, and our legal bases

We process the data above to:

- **Provide the service** — answer questions, generate recaps, read documents, and maintain saved context for a group.
- **Operate, secure, and improve the platform** — authentication, debugging, abuse prevention, and reliability.
- **Comply with legal obligations** and enforce our terms.

Where the GDPR or similar laws apply, our legal bases are:

- **Performance of a contract / provision of a requested service** — to operate the bot and dashboard for the Workspace and its users.
- **Legitimate interests** — to run, secure, and improve the service, provided these interests are not overridden by your rights. For group message processing, the Workspace Administrator (as controller) relies on its own lawful basis (typically legitimate interests or its relationship with group members); Leguan processes on their behalf.
- **Consent** — where specifically required. Where consent is the basis, you may withdraw it at any time.

We do **not** sell your personal data, and we do **not** use your group messages to train our own models.

---

## 4. AI model providers and how your messages are used

To generate answers, recaps, and document responses, Leguan sends the relevant message content, context documents, and your question to **third-party AI model providers** ("AI providers") that operate large language models via their APIs. These providers process the content solely to return a response to us; the content is not used by us to build a public profile of you.

We may use **more than one AI provider**, and the specific providers we use may change over time as we add or replace them. Some AI providers may be located in, or process data in, countries outside your own (see "International transfers" below). A Workspace Administrator who requires the current, specific list of AI and infrastructure sub-processors for their own compliance can request it from us at [privacy@leguan.ai].

## 5. Who else we share data with

Besides AI providers, we share data with:

- **Infrastructure sub-processors** — providers of cloud hosting, application deployment, and database services that host and run the platform on our behalf, under contractual data-protection obligations.
- **Legal and safety** — where required by law, or to protect the rights, safety, and security of users, the public, or Leguan.
- **Business transfers** — if Leguan is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, data may transfer as part of that transaction, subject to this policy.

We do not otherwise disclose your personal data to third parties for their own purposes.

## 6. International transfers

Leguan and its providers may process data in countries other than the one you live in, including countries whose data-protection laws differ from yours. Where we transfer personal data internationally in a way that triggers legal safeguards (for example, out of the EEA or UK), we take steps intended to ensure an appropriate level of protection, such as relying on recognized adequacy decisions or standard contractual clauses. For more detail on the safeguards applicable to a specific transfer, contact us at [privacy@leguan.ai].

## 7. How long we keep data

We keep personal data for as long as needed to provide the service to a Workspace and its groups, and to meet the purposes described in this policy.

- **Group messages and saved context** are retained while the bot remains active in the group and the underlying data is needed for its features, unless you or the Workspace Administrator request earlier deletion.
- **Dashboard account data** is retained while the account is active.
- **Operational logs** are retained for a limited period for security and debugging.

## 8. Your choices and rights

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights over your personal data: **access** a copy of it; **correct** inaccurate data; **delete** it; **object to** or **restrict** its processing; **withdraw consent** where processing is based on consent; and **data portability**.

Two choices are worth calling out for group members:

- **Excluding your messages from processing (opt-out).** You can ask for your messages to be excluded from logging and from the bot's features. Contact us at [privacy@leguan.ai] or ask a Workspace Administrator. [We are adding an in-chat command to make this self-service; until then, requests are handled manually.]
- **Deletion.** You can ask us to delete messages and associated data attributable to you.

To exercise any right, contact **[privacy@leguan.ai]**. Because Leguan is often a processor acting for a Workspace Administrator, we may need to route your request to, or confirm it with, the relevant administrator, and we will help you do so. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law. You also have the right to complain to your local data-protection authority.

**For California residents (CCPA/CPRA):** we do not sell or "share" (as defined for cross-context behavioral advertising) your personal information, and we do not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. The rights and contact method above apply to you.
